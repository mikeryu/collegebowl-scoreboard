{\huge\bf Toss-Up Question}




{\Large The slope of the line connecting the points $(1, 6)$ and $(5,x)$ is $\frac{3}{4}$. Find $x$.}





\vspace{0.3in}

{\huge\bf Answer}

{\Large $x=9$.}

{\huge\bf Follow-Up Question}

{\Large A biker bikes at a rate of 10 miles per hour for ten minutes and then rides at a constant speed of $x$ miles per hour for the remainder of an hour. If the biker's average velocity over the hour is $20$ miles per hour, find $x$.}\\


\vspace{0.3in}

{\huge\bf Answer}

{\Large $x=22$ miles per hour.}

{\huge\bf Toss-Up Question}





{\Large A sink can be filled by a faucet in $5$ minutes, but it takes $7$ minutes to drain a full sink. If the faucet is running and the drain is  open, how long will it take to fill the sink?}

\vspace{0.5in}

{\huge\bf Answer}

{\Large $17.5$ minutes OR $\frac{35}{2}$ minutes.}

\vspace{2in}

{\huge\bf Follow-Up Question}

{\Large If $4$ liters of a $20\%$ acid solution are mixed with $6$ liters of a $25\%$ acid solution, what is the concentration of the resulting solution?}

\vspace{0.5in}

{\huge\bf Answer}

{\Large $23\%$.}

%-------------------Question/Follow-up 3-------------------------


{\huge\bf Toss-Up Question}



{\Large Worker $A$ can complete a certain job in $4$ hours. Worker $B$ can complete the same job in $6$ hours. How long will it take them to complete the job if they work together?}

\vspace{0.5in}

{\huge\bf Answer}

{\Large $2.4$ hours OR $2\frac{2}{5}$ hours OR $2$ hours and $24$ minutes.}

\vspace{2in}

{\huge\bf Follow-Up Question}

{\Large Algy can paint a certain room in one hour less than Gebra. Together they can complete the job in one hour and $12$ minutes. How long would it take each of them to complete the job individually?}

\medskip



\vspace{0.5in}

{\huge\bf Answer}

{\Large Two hours for Algy and $3$ hours for Gebra.}

\clearpage

{\huge\bf Toss-Up Question}



{\Large A biker leaves at 8:00 a.m. traveling $10$ miles per hour. Using the same route, a driver leaves at 10:00 a.m. traveling at $60$ miles per hour. At what time will the driver catch up to the biker?}

\vspace{0.5in}

{\huge\bf Answer}

{\Large 10:24 am.}\ \\

{\huge\bf Follow-Up Question}

{\Large Runner $A$ and Runner $B$ are running around a small circular track with radius $6$ meters. If Runner A runs at a rate of 10 degrees per seconds and Runner B runs at a rate of $12$ degrees per second (both measured from the center of the circle), then how much more distance in meters will Runner B have covered than Runner A after $10$ seconds?}



\vspace{0.5in}

{\huge\bf Answer}

{\Large $\frac{2}{3}\pi$.}
